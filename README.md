## Inside the Bottle: A Breakdown of Every Active Ingredient

The supplement facts label lists 10 research-backed compounds. Here's what each one actually does — and what the published science says about it.

### 1. Niacinamide (200 mg) — the NAD+ booster

Niacinamide is a form of vitamin B3. Its main job in this formula is to raise NAD+ — a coenzyme your mitochondria literally cannot make ATP without. Your brain can't think without NAD+. Your heart can't beat without it. The sales page cites a striking claim: niacinamide can boost NAD+ levels "by 3,000% in just 30 minutes." That figure traces back to research showing niacinamide is one of the fastest-acting NAD+ precursors available without a prescription.

### 2. D-Ribose — the ATP building block

A five-carbon sugar that doesn't spike blood sugar. It's a structural component of ATP itself, so the logic is: give your mitochondria the bricks, they'll build more energy.

### 3. Itadori Extract (Polygonum cuspidatum) — the longevity switch

This is a source of trans-resveratrol, which activates sirtuins — proteins often called the "longevity enzymes." Sirtuins regulate how your cells handle stress and repair.

### 4. Pyrroloquinoline Quinone / PQQ (5 mg) — the new-mitochondria trigger

PQQ is the standout. A study in the *Journal of Biological Chemistry* showed PQQ activates genes involved in mitochondrial biogenesis — meaning it may help your body grow *new* mitochondria, not just feed the ones you have. The 5 mg dose here is modest compared to standalone PQQ supplements, but it works in synergy with the rest of the stack.

### 5. Acetyl-L-Carnitine (500 mg) — the fat shuttle

This amino-acid derivative ferries fatty acids into your mitochondria so they can be burned for energy. The 500 mg dose aligns with what clinical studies have used for age-related energy decline.

### 6. Coenzyme Q10 (50 mg) — the electron transport chain MVP

CoQ10 sits inside your mitochondrial membrane and helps electrons move down the chain that produces ATP. Natural CoQ10 production falls off a cliff with age — by some estimates, an 80-year-old has 40% less than a 20-year-old. The 50 mg here is a moderate, evidence-based dose.

### 7. R-Lipoic Acid (100 mg) — the antioxidant recycler

Note the "R" — this is the biologically active isomer, not the cheaper synthetic mix. Lipoic acid protects mitochondria from oxidative damage and recycles other antioxidants like vitamin C and E.

### 8. Quercetin + Curcumin + BioPerine — the absorption trio

Quercetin and curcumin are both polyphenol antioxidants. BioPerine (black pepper extract) is included because clinical trials show it boosts curcumin absorption **20-fold** and quercetin absorption **6-fold**. Without it, you'd be peeing out most of those expensive polyphenols.

### 9. Magnesium (malate form) — the ATP cofactor

Magnesium is required for ATP to actually be biologically active. The malate form is gentle on the stomach and pairs well with energy metabolism.

**Plain-language summary: this isn't a random vitamin grab-bag. Each ingredient maps onto a specific step in how mitochondria make energy, protect themselves, or multiply.**
